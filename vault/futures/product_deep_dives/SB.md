# [[SB]] — Sugar #11 (World Sugar)

## Contract specs

- Exchange / product: ICE Futures U.S. (formerly NYBOT), Sugar #11
- Tick size: 0.01 cents/pound; tick value: $11.20 per contract
- Contract multiplier: 112,000 pounds per contract
- Contract months: March, May, July, October (rolling through the year)
- Session hours: 2:30 AM ET — 1:00 PM ET (RTH open 9:30 AM ET)
- Last trading day: business day prior to the last business day of the delivery month
- Settlement: physical (raw sugar delivery in Carioca region, Brazil) or cash
- Margin at Topstep: ~$4K–$6K initial (verify in platform)

## What it actually is

Raw cane sugar futures: exposure to the global sugar market, with Brazil as the dominant producer (~40% of global supply). Used by refiners, exporters, blenders, and traders. Most trades are financial flows, not hedging; few take delivery. This is the benchmark for world raw sugar pricing. Highly influenced by Brazilian weather (harvest is Apr–Nov) and sugar-to-ethanol crush economics in Brazil's sucrose mills.

## Primary drivers

1. **Brazilian supply / weather** — Brazil's Center-South region harvest determines ~40% of global supply. Drought, frost, or delayed crushing = tight supply.
2. **Brazil ethanol crush economics** — if ethanol prices relative to sugar are high, mills switch more juice to ethanol rather than sugar, reducing global sugar supply.
3. **Global ethanol prices** — corn & sugarcane ethanol are fungible in Brazil; high ethanol = diversion of sugarcane.
4. **India production and exports** — India is #2 producer and exporter. Export restrictions (to support domestic prices) can tighten global supply.
5. **China demand** — largest importer; any Chinese stimulus or slowdown impacts global sugar demand.
6. **Technical fund positioning** — CTA trend-followers can drive multimulti-week rallies or collapses if positioned heavily one-directionally.

## Key correlations

- Positively correlated with: [[BZ]] (Brent), [[CL]] (crude) — energy complex. [[CC]] (cocoa) — softs complex. Ethanol prices (very tight).
- Negatively correlated with: [[ZC]] (corn, when ethanol crush is tight) — competing feedstock in Brazil.
- Lead/lag: Brazil harvest reports (May–Sep, weekly UNICA data) lead by 3–5 days. USDA crop updates lead by 1–2 days.

## Recurring patterns

- **Seasonal rally Apr–Jun** — anxiety about Brazilian harvest weather (dry season in Apr, frost risk in Jun).
- **Post-harvest collapse Jul–Aug** — if crop proves abundant, buyers step in after harvest fears ease.
- **Ethanol spike rallies** — when ethanol rallies hard (crude spikes), mills shift, sugar supply tightens, SB catches bid within 2–3 days.
- **Fund positioning cascades** — CTA trend-followers often stack heavily in sugar; reversals can be violent (5–8¢ overnight).
- **Mumbai sugar inventory surprise** — India's weekly sugar stock data can move SB 0.5–1.5¢ intraday if supply perception shifts.

## Common setups

1. **Harvest-weather scare long.** During Brazilian harvest (May–Sep), first drought/frost headline → long via bull call spread or outright with 2¢ stop. Exit on first break below 5-day MA or at +3R.
2. **Ethanol-crush carry.** When ethanol is >50¢/gal and trending up, mills divert more sugar. SB rallies; structure a directional long or calendar spread (near-month tight, deferred higher).
3. **Positioning unwind.** When large fund long positioning is detected via COT data spikes, watch for catalyst. Brazil supply report (UNICA) beats estimates → funds capitulate. Short-fade setup.
4. **Seasonal mean-reversion Jul-Aug.** After post-harvest collapse, support often holds; bounce plays work off oversold levels with 1.5¢ stop.

## Classic traps

- **Chasing ethanol rallies** — sugar lags ethanol and often reverses faster. Don't chase SB 3¢ higher because ethanol spiked; wait for confirmation.
- **Ignoring India policy shifts** — surprise export bans or domestic price controls can flip the market 3¢ overnight. Check WSJ / commodity news for India export announcements.
- **Harvest overconfidence** — one good rainfall report in Jun doesn't ensure a good harvest. Brazil's harvest is 6 months long; early optimism often fades.
- **Undercapitalizing for volatility** — SB can do 2–3¢ intraday on harvest stories. Tight stops get wicked out; respect the tape.
- **Holding over USDA reports** — April WASDE contains Brazil corn projections which affect ethanol crush; hold unhedged at your peril.

## Liquidity profile

- Average daily volume: 50K–150K contracts on front month; July is typically most liquid.
- Spreads: typically 0.02–0.05¢ (2–5 ticks) in tight flow; can widen to 0.10¢ around reports.
- Best fills: 10:00 AM — 12:00 PM ET (London overlap, when Brazilian traders are online).
- Pre-open (2:30–9:30 AM ET): thinner liquidity, wider spreads. Overnight holds risk gapping on Brazil news.

## Options (if applicable)

- Monthly expirations only (no weeklies).
- Settlement: American-style (exercise any time before expiry).
- Typical IV rank: 30–50 in calm regimes, 70–90 during harvest scares or fund capitulations.
- Put walls: often cluster at round 20¢ strike in bear markets; call walls higher in bull mode.

## Risk notes

- Gap risk: very real. Brazil harvest news overnight can gap 2–3¢ on open. Never hold unhedged into harvest season reports or Brazil-specific supply updates.
- Limit-up days: not common, but can happen during Brazil frost scares (Jan–Jun); 2 consecutive limit-up moves trigger trading halt.
- Worst weekly moves (last 5 years): +8¢ (Brazil drought 2023), −6¢ (harvest relief 2021).
- Tail-risk events: Brazil political instability, export bans, or trade wars; index fund capitulation (sugar is part of commodity indices).
