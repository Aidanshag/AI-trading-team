# [[6E]] — Euro FX

## Contract specs

- Exchange / product: CME, Euro FX futures (6E)
- Quote: USD per EUR (price of 1 EUR in USD); long 6E = long EUR / short USD
- Tick size: 0.00005; tick value: $6.25 per contract
- Contract multiplier: €125,000
- Contract months: Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec (serial months too, lower volume)
- Session hours: Sun 17:00 CT → Fri 16:00 CT, 1-hr daily break
- Last trading day: 9:16 AM CT on 2nd business day before 3rd Wednesday of contract month
- Settlement: physical (EUR delivery; nearly always rolled before delivery by retail)
- Margin at Topstep: ~$3K initial (verify)

Also see [[M6E]] (micro, 1/10 size). Same dynamics, smaller sizing.

## What it actually is

The euro expressed as a US-dollar futures contract. Remember: **this is NOT spot FX**. Price reflects EURUSD spot + forward points (interest-rate differential). Over time, forward points accumulate — but for day-to-day trading, it tracks EURUSD spot within basis-point noise.

## Primary drivers

1. **ECB vs Fed policy differential** — the dominant multi-month driver. Rate differential (DE 2Y − US 2Y) explains most of the level.
2. **Growth differential** — ZEW and IFO (Germany) vs ISM (US), PMIs, GDP.
3. **Energy shock transmission** — Europe imports energy; shocks (especially Russian gas) weigh on EUR.
4. **Risk-on/risk-off** — EUR tends to rally in risk-on; USD (as funding) outperforms in risk-off.
5. **Political/sovereign risk** — Italian debt, French elections, German fiscal debates periodically.
6. **ECB balance sheet moves** — QT pace, TLTRO repayments, LTRO operations.

## Key correlations

- Positively: [[6B]] (GBP, 0.7 correlation), risk-on equities, commodities (looser correlation), gold (dollar-weakness proxy).
- Negatively: [[DXY]] (by construction, but not perfectly — 6E is ~58% of DXY), US bond yields typically, risk-off assets.
- Cross-asset: EURUSD moves are often mirrored by EURCHF (CHF as funder) and EURGBP (relative EU dynamics).

## Recurring patterns

- **ECB Thursday** (monthly meeting): vol compresses into the 7:45 AM CT statement, expands at 8:30 AM CT press conference.
- **NFP Friday**: EUR moves inversely to USD, so US payrolls are effectively an EUR event.
- **European open (2:00 AM CT)**: liquidity ramps, London desk takes over at 3:00 AM CT.
- **US afternoon decay**: after 3:00 PM CT, flow thins and price drifts on rates.
- **Quarter-end fixings**: 10:00 AM CT London fix on quarter-end day produces flow-driven moves.

## Common setups

1. **Rate-differential pivot.** When US 2Y rolls over vs DE 2Y, 6E trends higher for 1-3 weeks. Wait for 5-day confirmation; enter with 1.5× ATR stop.
2. **ECB surprise.** Hawkish ECB + dovish Fed → sustained EUR bid over days. Position in direction of the surprise after the press conference if price confirms.
3. **Risk-on EUR.** In multi-day equity rallies, EUR often leads vs USD. Can pair with long ES for diversified risk-on expression (careful: it's one trade for sizing).
4. **Mean-revert around Draghi-era ranges.** If 6E is at the extremes of its 1Y range without a regime catalyst, partial mean-reversion trades are reasonable with tight stops.

## Classic traps

- **Ignoring US data for an EUR trade.** Half of 6E's move is USD. You must have a view on both sides.
- **Fighting ECB intervention.** Verbal intervention from ECB officials around EUR weakness can reverse trends quickly — listen to speech schedule.
- **Thin Asian hours.** 18:00-22:00 ET is thin; moves can be whippy and not signal anything.
- **Event stacking.** EU CPI + FOMC + NFP in the same week = noisy and full of traps; prefer defined-risk option structures.

## Liquidity profile

- Front-month volume: ~200K–350K contracts daily.
- Spread: 1 tick typical (sometimes half-tick in front).
- Best liquidity: 7:00 AM CT – 15:00 PM CT (European + US overlap).
- Overnight: decent Asian; best Frankfurt/London open.

## Options on /6E

- EUU weekly and monthly.
- European-style, cash-settled.
- IV regime: typically 5-8 vol, spikes to 12-15 around rate decisions.
- Common structure: risk-reversals around ECB/FOMC days, iron condors in grinding ranges.

## Risk notes

- Gap risk: moderate; weekend EU political risk can gap 30-50 pips.
- Worst weekly moves: 2022 saw EUR below parity with USD for the first time since 2002 (energy crisis + aggressive Fed tightening).
- Tail risk: EU sovereign crises (Italy, France periodically) can produce disorderly moves — watch Italian 10Y spreads to Bund.
- Intervention risk: ECB has less intervention history than BOJ/SNB, but Eurozone political risk occasionally forces coordination.
