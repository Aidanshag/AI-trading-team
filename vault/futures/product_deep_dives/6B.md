# [[6B]] — British Pound FX

## Contract specs

- Exchange / product: CME, British Pound futures (6B)
- Quote: USD per GBP (price of 1 GBP in USD); long 6B = long GBP / short USD
- Tick size: 0.0001; tick value: $6.25 per contract
- Contract multiplier: £62,500
- Contract months: Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec (serial months available, lower volume)
- Session hours: Sun 17:00 CT → Fri 16:00 CT, 1-hr daily break
- Last trading day: 2 business days before 3rd Wednesday of contract month
- Settlement: physical (GBP delivery; nearly always rolled before delivery by retail)
- Margin at Topstep: ~$2.5K initial (verify current)

Also see [[M6B]] (micro, 1/10 size) for tighter risk management.

## What it actually is

Sterling expressed as a USD-denominated futures contract. This is a highly liquid G10 FX pair and reflects the interest-rate and growth differential between the UK and US, plus risk-on/risk-off flows. Unlike spot, the futures contract carries forward points (the interest differential), but for intraday and multi-day trading, 6B tracks GBPUSD spot within basis-point noise.

## Primary drivers

1. **BoE vs Fed policy differential** — the structural anchor. Rate differential (UK 2Y − US 2Y) is the primary multi-month signal. When the Fed tightens ahead of BoE, GBP weakens; when BoE accelerates rate cuts less aggressively than Fed, GBP typically underperforms.
2. **UK growth and employment data** — OIS markets overfocus on wage growth (Average Earnings Index) as the leading inflation signal. Strong payroll data → BoE hawkish → GBP bid; weak unemployment claims → growth scare → GBP weakness.
3. **Risk-on/risk-off appetite** — GBP is a "risky" G10 currency (more pro-cyclical than EUR or CHF). Risk rallies favor GBP; risk-off episodes drive USD funding demand and GBP weakness.
4. **UK fiscal constraints and gilt issuance** — gilts are less liquid than US Treasuries; large auctions or fiscal surprise can pressure GBP via carry costs; gilt yields often lead GBPUSD by 2-5 days.
5. **Brexit-related supply-chain and export weakness** — structural headwind for GBP. UK trade data disappointments (especially goods exports) weigh GBP vs peers; energy import inflation hits harder than US.
6. **BoE communication granularity** — MPC is talkative and frequent guidance creates whipsaws. "Patients" on rate cuts flip to "data-dependent" overnight; listen to voting records on every decision.

## Key correlations

- Positively: [[6E]] (EUR, ~0.65 correlation; both G10 and rate-sensitive), risk-on equities, commodities (loose), cross-GBP pairs (GBPJPY, GBPCHF).
- Negatively: [[DXY]] (by construction, but weaker than 6E because GBP weight is lower in DXY), longer-dated gilt yields (when gilts spike up, carry dries; GBP weakens), USD in risk-off.
- Lead/lag: UK jobs data (released Tuesdays 4:30 AM ET) often leads GBPUSD by 30 min to 2 hours; BoE rate decision (10:00 AM GMT) is an instant repricing event with 1-3 min lag in futures.

## Recurring patterns

- **UK jobs Tuesdays (4:30 AM ET)**: AEI beats → potential GBP bid; misses → risk-off and GBP weakness. Volatility can persist 4-6 hours.
- **BoE decision days (10:00 AM GMT / 5:00 AM ET)**: vol compresses into the decision, then explodes. Best execution typically 5-15 min post-decision when algos finish repricing. Avoid the first 2 min (wide spreads).
- **US NFP (first Friday)**: USD strength from good data tends to hammer GBP more than EUR because GBP has lower carry benefit. If NFP is mixed (weak payrolls but strong wage growth), GBP can rally.
- **London open (2:00-3:00 AM CT)**: liquidity ramps; Asian thin spots get filled; spreads tighten.
- **FOMC decision (also an event for GBP because it tilts the BoE-Fed differential)**: FOMC hawkish → Fed-BoE gap widens → GBP sold.
- **Month-end and quarter-end fixing (London 10:00 AM GMT)**: order flow through spot can move GBP 20-50 pips in either direction.
- **Seasonal weakness in Aug/early Sep**: summer thinness and UK holiday season; bid-ask widens.

## Common setups

1. **Rate-differential reversal.** When UK 2Y > US 2Y (carry flip), or Fed slowing + BoE holding, GBP can enter a 2-3 week rally. Wait for 3-day higher-high pattern on daily; enter after BoE confirmation; target previous swing high; stop below recent lows (1.5× ATR).
2. **AEI surprise + trend confirmation.** Beat expectations for AEI → BoE hawkish sentiment shift → multi-day GBP bid. Confirm with break above 20-day moving average; exit on any disappointing retail or manufacturing data; stop just below support.
3. **Risk-on GBP carry.** In multi-week risk rallies (e.g., equity VIX < 15, no geopolitical shocks), GBP often leads vs JPY and CHF because of higher nominal yields. Can pair with long equities (ES) as a diversified risk-on hedge; stop if risk trades start breaking (VIX > 18).
4. **Gilt auction reaction.** Post-auction, if gilts rally (yields fall), GBP often strengthens over 1-3 days as carry costs ease. Weak gilt auction (high tail-risk ratio) → immediate GBP weakness and multi-day drag.

## Classic traps

- **Ignoring the BoE vocal majority.** UK media spends enormous energy on individual MPC member voting records. If 5 out of 9 are voting to hold, and you're long GBP expecting a cut, you're fighting the consensus; position sizing discipline matters.
- **UK election / political uncertainty.** UK politics move in cycles. Pre-election periods see GBP weakness as investors de-risk gilt duration; post-election rallies are common but sharp and reversible. Watch polling and manifesto announcements.
- **Carry-trade unwinding in risk-off.** GBP is used as a carry-trade funding currency (less than JPY, but still). In sudden risk-off (e.g., geopolitical shock), GBP sell-offs can be more violent than EUR or AUD because GBP shorts get squeezed into higher yields.
- **UK data misses and forecast surprise.** UK data is volatile month-to-month (small sample size, revisions). A single PMI miss or wage-growth surprise can whipsaw the market for 2-3 days; avoid overweighting single releases.
- **Thin Asian hours.** 18:00-22:00 ET is thin and Tokyo handoff is choppy; spreads widen, moves are noisy. Prefer to enter/exit during London open or NY hours.

## Liquidity profile

- Front-month volume: ~150K–250K contracts daily (lower than 6E, but highly liquid).
- Spread: 1 tick typical during London-NY overlap; 2-3 ticks overnight.
- Best execution: 6:00 AM ET – 15:00 ET (London + NY hours).
- Asian hours: thinner; 1-2 spread typical but size available is smaller.
- Month-end effects: minor; GBP is a major pair so roll week is smooth.

## Options on /6B

- UK pound futures options (6BU, 6BZ quarterly).
- American-style, cash-settled in USD per GBP.
- IV regime: typically 7-9 vol, spikes to 12-16 around BoE decisions or major UK data (AEI, CPI).
- Common structures: straddles around BoE/FOMC, risk-reversals on rate-differential themes, iron condors in consolidation phases.

## Risk notes

- **Gap risk**: moderate; UK weekend political headlines (snap elections, budget surprise, Brexit-related policy) can gap GBPUSD 50-100 pips.
- **Worst weekly moves**: 2016 post-Brexit vote saw GBP drop >1000 pips over one session; 2022 saw Liz Truss fiscal shock produce 150+ pip moves in a single day.
- **Intervention risk**: BoE rarely intervenes, but watches GBP/EUR and GBP/JPY closely for financial-stability concerns; verbal intervention from BoE Governor can reverse intraday trends.
- **Gilt-market stress**: If UK gilt auction fails or gilts collapse (2022 pension-fund crisis), GBP can experience disorderly moves as carry unravels and foreign flows exit.
- **Brexit-related supply shocks**: irregular but impactful; check SVAR (Single Vehicle Approval) and freight data for hidden GBP weakness signals.
