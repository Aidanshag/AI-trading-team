# [[GC]] — Gold

## Contract specs

- Exchange / product: COMEX, Gold Futures (GC)
- Tick size: $0.10/troy oz; tick value: $10.00 per contract
- Contract multiplier: 100 troy oz
- Contract months: active Feb, Apr, Jun, Aug, Oct, Dec (Jun & Dec most liquid)
- Session hours: Sun 18:00 ET → Fri 17:00 ET, 1-hr daily break
- Last trading day: 3rd-last business day of contract month
- First notice day: last business day of preceding month
- Settlement: physical (Good Delivery bars)
- Margin at Topstep: ~$11K initial (verify)

Also see [[MGC]] (micro, 1/10 size).

## What it actually is

The world's senior monetary metal. Dual-use: industrial (small), financial reserve (large), jewelry. Central banks are structural buyers; ETF flows are pro-cyclical with macro fear. Not an income-producing asset — opportunity cost is real yields (10Y TIPS).

## Primary drivers

1. **Real yields (10Y TIPS)** — the single dominant driver over long horizons. Gold rises when real yields fall.
2. **USD (DXY)** — inverse correlation typically −0.4 to −0.6.
3. **Central-bank buying** — structural tailwind since 2022 (de-dollarization narrative). Not a short-term signal but a floor.
4. **Geopolitical risk** — haven flows during crises; reverses post-event.
5. **Inflation expectations** — breakevens and CPI prints; gold hedges unexpected inflation.
6. **ETF flows** (GLD, IAU) — pro-cyclical; positioning signal.

## Key correlations

- Positively: [[SI]] (gold-silver ratio mean-reverts; silver higher beta), Swiss franc (haven), long-duration bonds at times.
- Negatively: [[DXY]], 10Y TIPS real yield, crypto in most risk-on periods.
- Cross-asset: gold/silver ratio rising = defensive; falling = risk-on.

## Recurring patterns

- **FOMC drift** — gold rallies into a dovish surprise, fades into hawkish. Event-window rules apply.
- **Asian demand seasons** — Chinese New Year (Jan-Feb), Indian wedding season (Oct-Nov). Physical demand builds into these.
- **Crisis flights** — sharp risk-off weeks see gold spike then consolidate.
- **Year-end repositioning** — portfolio rebalancing flows can push metals in Dec.

## Common setups

1. **Real-yield pivot trade.** 10Y TIPS rolling over from a cycle high → long gold with 1.5× ATR stop; ride to 2–3R or until TIPS bounce.
2. **FOMC expectations shift.** If the market is pricing in a hawkish path and macro data comes in soft, position long gold 24-48h before the next Fed communication.
3. **Gold-silver ratio mean reversion.** Ratio > 85 historically favors silver (short GSR); < 65 favors gold. Position as paired spread.
4. **Event-driven haven play.** Defined-risk option structure ahead of geopolitical flashpoints (avoid outright entries due to news-driven reversals).

## Classic traps

- **Chasing geopolitical spikes.** The first 24h of a geopolitical rally is usually the highest price for weeks. Don't chase.
- **Fighting the trend in regime pivots.** When real yields are falling in a sustained trend, don't short gold even if it looks overbought.
- **Inflation-hedge narrative fallacy.** Gold hedges UNEXPECTED inflation, not realized-and-fought inflation. 2022 showed this — rising CPI with aggressive Fed hikes = gold flat/down despite high inflation.
- **Liquidity trap.** GC is liquid; options can be thin at non-front strikes. Check before entry.

## Liquidity profile

- Front-month volume: ~200K–400K contracts daily.
- Spread: 1 tick typical.
- Best liquidity: 8:00–17:00 ET.
- Overnight: thinner; wider spreads during Asian handoff.

## Options on /GC

- OG, weekly and monthly expirations.
- American-style.
- IV regime: typically 12-20 vol, spikes to 25-35 in crisis.
- IV rank matters more for gold than most commodities — the distribution is narrower.
- Common structure: long-premium directional plays around Fed events (IV is cheap relative to other commodities); iron condors in grinding low-vol periods.

## Risk notes

- Gap risk: moderate; weekend geopolitical news can produce 1-2% gaps.
- No price limits in normal conditions.
- Worst weekly moves: 2008 crisis saw 10%+ swings; 2020 Covid crash saw a 10% weekly drawdown before rally.
- Tail risk: central-bank policy pivots (unexpected hawkish) can produce cascade selling as positioning unwinds.
