## Lessons (potential, watch for confirmation)

### Pre-deploy R-multiple optimization is a trap
We almost shipped a parameter change suggested by cowork at the last minute. The change was based on R-multiples (target/stop ratio) without modeling slippage in dollars. **R-multiples can look great on a strategy whose absolute dollar edge is tiny — slippage destroys it.** Lesson: any optimization-mode recommendation must include slippage-adjusted dollar metrics, not just R-multiples.

If this pattern repeats — agent surfaces "improvement" that ignores friction — it becomes a RULE-tier lesson.

### The "autonomous loop" was overpromised on Friday
I told the user Friday night "the brain is continuously learning." It mostly is — git/memory/lessons all persist across sessions — but **cowork doesn't fire on timers.** Only the cloud routine does, and it appears silent today. Lesson: be precise about what's automatic vs user-triggered when describing system capabilities.
